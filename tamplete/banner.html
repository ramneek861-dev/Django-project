How Does
URL Scanner Work?

URL scanning refers to the process of analyzing URLs (Uniform Resource Locators) for potential security threats, malicious content, or indicators of compromise. This process is typically performed by specialized security tools or services designed to detect and mitigate web-based threats. Here's an overview of how URL scanning works in the context of Intelliguard cyber:


URL Collection

The URL scanning process begins with the collection of URLs to be scanned. These URLs can come from various sources, including website logs, email messages, social media, user submissions, or threat intelligence feeds.


Detection of Threats

URL scanners detect threats, vulnerabilities,and suspicious activity.Common threats include malware hosting phishing drive-by downloads and malicious redirectsto harmful websites or phishing pages.

URL Analysis

Each collected URL is subjected to analysis to determine its reputation, trustworthiness, and potential security risks. This analysis may involve several techniques: Reputation Lookup
,Content Inspection,Behavioral Analysis


Risk Assessment

Each identified threat may be assigned a risk score or severity level based on factors such as the nature of the threat, its potential impact, and the likelihood of exploitation. This helps prioritize responses and mitigation efforts.



Reporting and Remediation

URL scan results compiled into report with threat details, affected URLs, recommended actions, and context. Remediation actions vary based on threat severity.
