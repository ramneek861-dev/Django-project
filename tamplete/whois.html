How Does
WHOIS Work?

WHOIS, an Internet protocol, works by querying databases containing registration details of domain names, IP addresses, and autonomous system numbers. It enables users to retrieve information about the entities responsible for registering internet resources, including contact details, registration dates, and administrative statuses. Here's an overview of how WHOIS works in the context of Intelliguard cyber:


Identifying Ownership

WHOIS helps cybersecurity professionals identify the owners of domain names and IP addresses, which is crucial for investigating security incidents and attributing responsibility, enabling effective communication for resolution.


Cyber-Sleuthing

WHOIS data can be used to identify suspicious domains used for phishing or fraudulent activities. Security teams can monitor newly registered domains for signs of malicious intent and take preventive measures accordingly.

Tracking Malicious Activities

By querying WHOIS databases, security analysts can track down the registration details of potentially malicious domains or IP addresses involved in cyber attacks, aiding in threat intelligence efforts.


Cyber-Forensics

Cybersecurity researchers leverage WHOIS to investigate the infrastructure of threat actors,such as their domain registration patterns,network affiliations,reveals threat actors infrastructure to uncover larger cybercrime.



Compliance and Regulations

WHOIS data is often required for compliance with regulations such as GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and ICANN (Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers) policies.
